London

Known as one of the world's major global cities, London exerts a strong influence on world art, entertainment, fashion, commerce, finance, education, healthcare, media, science, technology, tourism, transport, and communications. As the capital and largest city of the United Kingdom, it hosts Europe's largest concentration of higher education institutions and is home to several of the world's leading academic institutions. Additionally, the diverse culture encompasses over 300 languages, making London Europe's third-most populous city.

Top Attractions

No trip to London is complete without visiting the Elisabeth Tower, the tall clock tower next to House of Parliament. It lays next to the River Thames with the giant ferris wheel London Eye on the other side of the river. The breathtaking view from the ferris wheel over all London is unforgetable.

Another must-visit is the Buckingham Palace. Even though the palace itself is only open to the public during a limited number of months in the summer, the change of guards in front of the palace is a great experience, especially for children. Also, the Tower Bridge with the Tower of London next to it is a perfect mixture of history and innovations.

Food & Drink

While the British kitchen isn't known for outstanding dishes, there are some must-trys in London. The first dish is Fish and Chips. It is a quintessential British dish, and very popular as street food. Another famous dish is a traditional Full English Breakfast, including eggs, bacon, sausages, beans, and toast. Another favourite for British people is the Sunday Roast, a savory meal with roasted meat, potatoes, vegetables, and gravy. The best place to enjoy these dishes is in a traditional pub or on street markets, such as Camden Market.

Culture & Festivals

The urban culture of London includes music, theatre, festivals, and museums. There are three major concert halls in London: the Barbican Hall, the Royal Festival Hall, and the Royal Albert Hall. Additionally, London has a famous rock music scene, and some of the greatest artists of the 60s and 70s played in London. Until today the music scene is vibrating, especially in places like Covent Garden, where street artists show of their creative abilities. The city is also known for its theatre quarter with the West End, but London is also home to the Shakespeare's Globe. Throughout the year, there are over 40 festivals per year in London. The most famous one being the Notting Hill Carnival with a distinctly Afro-Caribbean vibe. Furthermore, London is home to over 240 museums and galleries. The most famous ones being the British Museum and Tate Modern.

Travel Tips

The best time to visit London is between May and July, when the summer weather brings the city to life, even though one should never forget an umbrella when visiting London. English is the official language, and the local currency is the British Pound (£). While London is generally safe for tourists, it’s smart to stay aware of your belongings, especially in crowded areas. Pickpockets use big crowds to steal money from especially tourists.
