Chongqing

Known as the "Mountain City" and the "Fog Capital", Chongqing is a major city in southwest China. Famous for its stunning night skyline, spicy hotpot, and position along the Yangtze River, Chongqing is a vibrant hub that combines modern skyscrapers with deep cultural history.

Top Attractions

Don’t miss the Yangtze River Cable Car, one of the city’s most iconic experiences, offering sweeping views across the river and skyline. For nature lovers, visit Ciqikou Ancient Town, a riverside neighborhood with traditional tea houses and old architecture.

Other highlights include Hongya Cave, a cliffside complex lit up beautifully at night, and Dazu Rock Carvings, a UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing Buddhist sculptures.

Food & Drink

Chongqing is the birthplace of Hotpot, a must-try experience with its spicy, numbing broth made from Sichuan peppercorns. Street food is also a highlight — try xiaomian (spicy noodles), chuan chuan (skewers cooked in hotpot broth), and local snacks like glutinous rice dumplings.

Culture & Festivals

Chongqing is rich in cultural traditions and modern lifestyle. The city celebrates the Spring Festival with lantern shows and dragon dances. Being a hotpot capital, there’s even a Hotpot Festival where locals and tourists gather to enjoy the city’s most famous dish.

Travel Tips

The best time to visit Chongqing is from March to May or September to November, when the weather is mild. The city is known for its fog and humid summers, so prepare accordingly. The local dialect is Chongqing Mandarin, though Mandarin Chinese (Putonghua) is widely spoken. The currency is the Chinese Yuan (CNY).
